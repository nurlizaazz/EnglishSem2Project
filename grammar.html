Present Simple:

Simple Present Tense is a form of tense that is used to express events that occur regularly, routinely, habitually, or are usually done in the present.

Nominal Sentences
  • (+) Subject + To be + Complement
  • (-) Subject + To be + Not + Complement
  • (?) To be + Subject + Complement
Verbal Sentences
  • (+) Subject + Verb 1 (s/es) + Complement
  • (-) Subject + Do/Does Not + Verb 1 + Complement
  • (?) Do/Does + Subject + Verb 1 + Complement
Example :
Active Sentences
  • I bathe my cat in the bathtub every weekend.
  • I do homework every evening.
  • She calls her mother to help her.
  • Mother scolds Edward because he forgot to close the garage door.
Passive Sentences
  • The cat is bathed every weekend.
  • Homework is done every evening.
  • Her mother is called to help her.
  • Edward is scolded by Mother because the garage door is left open.

Simpe Past Tense :

Simple past tense is a sentence used to tell about events that happened in the past and finished or ended in the past too.

Nominal Sentences
  • (+) Subject + to be (was/were) + complement
  • (-) Subject + To be (was/were) + Not + Complement
  • (?) To be (was/were) + Subject + Complement
Verbal Sentences
  • (+) Subject + Verb 2 + Complement
  • (-) Subject + Did Not + Verb 1 + Complement
  • (?) Did + Subject + Verb 1 + Complement
Example :
Active Sentences
  • She was happy yesterday.
  • Was he in the office yesterday?
  • They built a new bridge last year.
  • She didn't bake a cake yesterday.
  • Did he write the letter?
Passive Sentences
  • A new bridge did built by them last year.
  • The cake didn't bake by here.
  • A delicious meal was prepared by the chef.
  • She bought a new car yesterday.

Simpe Future Tense :

Simple past tense is a sentence used to tell about events that happened in the past and finished or ended in the past too.

Formula :
  • (+) Subject + will + Verb 1 + complement
  • (-) Subject + will + Not + Verb 1 + Complement
  • (?) Will + Subject + V1 + Complement
  • (+) Subject +To be (is/am/are) + going to + Verb 1 + Complement
  • (-) Subject + To be (is/am/are) + not + Going to + Verb 1 + Complement
  • (?) To be (is/am/are) + Subject + Going to + Verb 1 + Complement
Example :
Active Sentences
  • She will study tonight.
  • I will not go to the party.
  • Will you come with us?
  • They are going to visit grandma.
  • He is not going to sleep late.
  • Are you going to attend the Meeting.
Passive Sentences
  • The lesson will be studied tonight by her.
  • The party will not to be attended by them.
  • Will you be invited to come with us?
  • Grandma is going to be visited by me.
  • We are not going to be joined by him.
  • Is the meeting going to be attended by you?

Present Continuous Tense :

Continuous tense is a form of tense that you can use to explain somthing or express an action that is taking place during a certain time in the present.

Formula :
Active voice
  • (+) Subject + to be (is/am/are) + Verb-ing + complement
  • (-) Subject + To be (is/am/are) + Not + Verb-ing + Complement
  • (?) To be (is/am/are) + Subject + Verb-ing + Complement
Passive Voice
  • (+) Subject + to be (is/am/are) + being + Verb 3 + Complement
  • (-) Subject + to be (is/am/are) + not + being + Verb 3 + Complement
  • (?) to be (is/am/are) + Subject + being + Verb 3 + Complement
Example :
Active Sentences
  • I am eating breakfast.
  • They are not playing football.
  • Is she cleaning the room?
Passive Sentences
  • The breakfast is being eaten by me.
  • Football is not being played by them.
  • Is the room being cleaned?

Present Prefect Tense :

Present perfect is used to show the relationship between the present and the past. The time of the action or event is before now, but not specific, and we are often more interested in the outcome or consequences than in the action it self. Formula :

Active voice
  • (+) Subject + has/have + Verb 3 + complement
  • (-) Subject + has/have + Not + Verb 3 + Complement
  • (?) Has/have + Subject + Verb 3 + Complement
Passive Voice
  • (+) Subject + has/have + been + Verb 3 + Complement
  • (-) Subject + has/have + not + been + Verb 3 + Complement
  • (?) Has/have + Subject + been + Verb 3 + Complement
Example :
Active Sentences
  • She has finished her homework.
  • They have not eaten dinner.
  • Have you seen this movie?
Passive Sentences
  • The homework has been finished by her.
  • Dinner has not been eaten by them.
  • Has this movie been seen by you?
